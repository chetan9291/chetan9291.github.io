EXPENSE MANAGER


“Beware of little expenses. A small leak will sink a great ship.” 

-Benjamin Franklin





It’s quite easy to lose track of expenditures. People have been struggling with expenses ever since they are capable of spending. Documentation of these habits have developed from pen and paper to app these days. 



The Challenge

The following project was done for a competition. The brief was to design a simple record app interface with the basic function below whereby users are able to: 1 Record their daily expenses 2 View a summary of their expenses by daily/monthly 3 Select category of their expenses The requirement was to draw the ideas of the interface with no restriction on any ideas for the app interface. The deliverables included wireframes, flowchart/storyboard of the app, and 2 visual designs with the integration of the style guide to the UI designed. This was to be completed within 4 days.



The Approach

As the information provided was limited, the approach was to gather more information and making assumptions that would guide design decisions. Time constraint lead to selective adoption of UX methodologies to come to the deliverables. The vision was to present an app that would be usable, interactive and user friendly while performing the basic functions stipulated above.





Discovery

I started with Competitor Analysis. These provided insight about the target audience, necessary features and the relevance of add-on features.



Persona

A pseudo-persona was developed that helped map the opportunities. These created the direction of the app. 






The Framework

The research and analysis done before, guided the structure of content and interaction of the app. For a first time user, the registration encompasses personal information, account information, permission to sync automatically through messages, define income sources, categories of expenditure and define recurring bills.






Prototyping





The transactions have a section for “Reimbursible Expenses” where the user can sort the expenses and also update their status. The user can verify the card charges to add or not if need be. Expenses can also be added manually.





An intuitive budget is set for primary categories by the app. This can be customized by the user. The user can also see the summary and details of all expenses in the categories.





The app also acts as a decision making tool. With its “Shop or not?” feature, the user can ask the feasibility of buying a product in some category. It will present the possible solutions or adjustments that can make the purchase viable.



There is no guarantee to cater to all the categories of expenditure of a user. Hence, there is the option of adding new categories besides the one present.





In addition to expenditure reports, users can also see the forecast of their expenses. The app alerts them and predicts estimation on the basis of past expenses.



The Design






F
The visual design communicates clarity where information content is conveyed quickly and directly. Important details, actionable items are distinguished with accent color to direct attention.
Attenuated and flat icons have been used in combination with bright and visible colors having befitting contrast. The UI enforces comprehensibility and conforms to the user expectations.






This app was designed with the motive to create a simple and intuitive app interface focusing on millenials. The features were a calculated selection tailored to their needs and comfort.
