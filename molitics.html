MOLITICS


August 2016 saw the launch of India’s first ever political awareness app for the public. This was a journey where I made a positive contribution in everyday lives. It also had a leader’s version that helped them to have an updated digital organization.



My Role

I lead the UX work from client interactions to producing all the major deliverables. I was responsible for the experience strategy and design of both the Android apps.



The Challenge

To develop a political brand that helps public and leaders in their political activities. Hence “Molitics” was born. 

There were two aspects of the brand. One that catered to the general public and the other that was to be made for the political leaders that would help them with management. Both were to be presented to the public in form of apps within 4 months (strategy to development). 





THE PUBLIC APP: MOLITICS

Molitics stands for "Media of Politics”. The vision is to become the medium for all political news and raise awareness.

    View App    


The Approach

Through multiple stakeholder’s meetings, we knew we were not only presenting something new for the masses but also competing with all the major news channels for a space on their phones. We started with developing meaningful goals for the users. 

For the first phase of the app, we decided to earn trust and customer base by implementing basics brilliantly. Also establish connection through user participation and interaction.



Discovery

In this phase I defined the client’s objectives and started with our user research. The research was centred around user needs, behaviours and pain points.I decided to adopt a Lean UX approach due to time and budget constraints. I created our primary personas, one to four. They were used throughout to guide design decisions, priorities and create empathy. The process has been shown below.



Our Customers

Two types of primary customers were identified based on localization and their traits were mapped. With their demographic information, the user research provided menaningful insights.








Personas

The four major groups of our users are represented with this persona chart. They describe real people with backgrounds, goals and values. With a clarity on the user’s expectations, the universal features anf functionalities are penned down.





Our Analysis

Through our personas, I could visualise our users experience.I used experience mapping to map out their journey and gather expectation, opportunities and pain points.





This story of customer’s experience, helped identify key interactions and the value they are adding.





Information Architecture

After approval of content and functionality, I started structuring the content.





Mobile Prototyping

I started with sketching the wireframes to give a canvas to the structure and content. Through the wireframes, the interaction of one screen to another was portrayed.





The Design




Expanding on our insights, the structure paved way to the final design. The user was greeted with the national and international political news on login. Access to regional political news became extremely easy with the use of location. The leaders can be seen according to region and constituency, with an option to follow their political activities.





The opinions of people can be heard on a colossal platform through surveys. These ask political questions and get answers from a politically informed audience. This app also addresses to the challenge of “limited knowledge of english” of its users. It’s has a bilingual setting and available in the user’s native language, hindi.





Launch

The app was launched in a political rally in Haryana,India. We presented this app to the crowd of supporters explaining its usability and features. Within two days we had 500+ downloads on the app store.  As of now, the app is a work in progress. We are continually conducting usability testing to improve its features and validate our assumptions. 





Impact

The app has proved it’s usefulness for politically keen users. The concept and content have been positively received. With continous useful feedbacks, the app will move on to another phase by the mid of 2017.

THE POLITICAL APP: MOLITICS PLUS

Two months after the public app, this app was launched for politicians in October 2016. This focused on equipping the leaders with modern technology to organise their day-to-day activities. 

    View App    


The Approach

After contextual enquiry of daily activities done by the leaders, I had clarity about the important features needed. I also realised that the app is likely to be used by their assistants more than the politicians. This resulted in the creation of two personas and balancing their needs. 



Discovery

Our stakeholders were influential and visionary politicians. Meetings and interviews with them provided us the canvas for persona creation.





Personas

With our interviews, it was evident that along with leaders, there will also be their personal assistants (maybe multiple) using this app.





Analysis

Considering the various scenarios, I mapped out the user’s feelings, questions and needs.



Information Architecture

I structured their interaction and content on the basis of the findings. The wireframes were created that paved the way for final designs.







The Users

The need of creating a personalized community was catered through the functionaliy of adding users in the settings. Here, the leaders can add their assitants to disperse roles. The leader controls what functions can be performed by each assistant.









The Design

The visual language of this app was kept consistent with that of the public app to reflect the brand identity.







Launch

This app was an enterprise app wherein its apt was available to all leaders that joined the “Molitics” network. Over 100 leaders downloaded this apk in a month and started using it. We are dedicated to experience enhancement. The second version of this app is scheduled to release in February 2017.
