  • DOLL MUSEUM     Visual identity redesign

    Shankar’s International Dolls Museum has one of the largest collection of costume dolls ranking 10th in the world. Each doll is handcrafted after meticulous research into the physical attributes, dress and jewelry of individual characters according to its country and history. This project was to improve on the visual identity and signage language of the doll museum. Despite being world renowned, the knowledge about this museum is surprisingly low amongst masses. Even in the vicinity of the museum, appropriate directional signage is missing to indicate its existence.

  • POSTERS & GRAPHICS     A sneak peak





    This poster is a symbolic representation of the ideologies of “Gandhi” and “Krishna”. The peacock feather of Krishna, draws the silhouette of Gandhi and the untold stories of awakening and guidance.






    Both Gandhi and Krishna were revered leaders for their fight against injustice and oppression. The analogous flute of Krishna has replaced the walking stick of Gandhi to portray their metonym qualities.






    The story of Indian culture is woven in its textiles and motifs. This is a depiction of the wisdom of sages and the history of traditions.
